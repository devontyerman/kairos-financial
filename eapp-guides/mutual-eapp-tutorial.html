  • SECONDARY ADDRESSEE. A Secondary Addressee is someone other than the insured / owner that can receive notifications that the policy is in danger of cancelling or lapsing.

  • DRIVER'S LICENSE. A Driver's License is not needed for Final Expense (It helps for identification so obtain DL information if possible) however, not having a license may be a red flag for a Term or IUL application. They will ask additional questions as to why a the client does not have a license.

  • WILL THE PROPOSED INSURED BE THE OWNER? To keep it simple, try to keep the insured and owner the same. However sometimes someone may want coverage on a loved one (Child getting a policy on the parent to cover funeral expenses for example).

  • Click "NEXT".

  •  Complete Insurance History Section. IF the client has any recent or current policies with Mutual, it will find it and disclose it here. It will ask if the applicant will allow you to view the information. Once they give you permission, you can click "EDIT" and it will allow you to answer if the current application will replace existing or recent coverage on file. Even after completing existing coverage with Mutual, you will need to reenter the existing or recent coverage below where it asks about coverage details.

  • IF no coverage, select "NO" on both and then click "NEXT".

IMPORTANT NOTE FOR IULs: For IUL (Left), on the drop down with the label that says "PERMANENT LIFE", make sure that the selection is "Indexed Universal Life Express WITH EASY SOLVE". If the applicant is applying for the LEVEL coverage, but at this point shows that they may qualify for GRADED, you will need to let the client know the change in rates and policy specifics.

  • FIRST, IF client shows a Favorable Pre-approval, click "LOCK DATA & CONTINUE APPLICATION".

  • SECOND, click "NEXT".

  • NEXT, complete the health questionnaire and review the answers in the summary.

  • NOTE for IUL/Term applications: Applicant may be denied due to unemployment apart from being a student, or occupation in Trucking, Transportation, Gas / Oil, Law Enforcement and Military. You may choose "Administration" if they happen to work as a supporting role in these types of companies or occupations.

  • Click "CLICK HERE TO ADD" to add a primary beneficiary. If there are more than one beneficiary, the percentage with all combined must equal 100%. For example, if there are two, then the percentage for both should be something like 50/50 or 60/40. If three, 34/33/33.  Same applies to Contingent. NOTE: A "Contingent" is a back up beneficiary if the primary cannot receive the death benefit. It is not mandatory to add a contingent. A contingent can be added later by the applicant.

  • IMPORTANT: Beneficiary must be 18 years of age and over.
